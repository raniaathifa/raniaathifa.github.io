The Facts

The Indonesian capital of Jakarta is home to 10 million people but it is also one of the fastest-sinking cities in the world. If this goes unchecked, parts of the megacity could be entirely submerged by 2050. The dramatic rate at which Jakarta is sinking is partly down to the excessive extraction of groundwater but when groundwater is pumped out, the land above it sinks as if it is sitting on a deflating balloon - and this leads to land subsidence. The solution of this problem would be to halt all groundwater extraction and solely rely on other sources of water, such as river water, but since almost all of the river in Jakarta are not worth using because the water are too filthy we have to clean it aggressively and Jakarta must do this by 2050 to avoid major subsidence.
